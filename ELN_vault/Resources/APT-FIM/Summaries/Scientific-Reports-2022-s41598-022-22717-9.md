# Scientific-Reports-2022-s41598-022-22717-9

## Overview
This study demonstrates the capability of Atom Probe Tomography (APT) to perform 3D nanoscale reconstruction and chemical mapping of frozen "soft" matter, specifically using natural honey as a model for complex biological/natural matrices. The research focuses on identifying phase separation, quantifying water content, and characterizing nano-precipitates within an aqueous organic solution without dehydration or melting.

## Key Findings
* **Phase Separation:** Identified two distinct phases in frozen honey: a crystalline phase (lower water content, $\sim$23%) and a liquid phase (higher water and sugar concentration, $\sim$33%). 
* **Compositional Analysis:** The crystalline phase was found to have a sugar concentration of $77.45 \pm 1.24\%$. Total water content was quantified between 18.7–36.5 wt% using protonated water clusters $(H_2O)_nH^+$ as chemical fingerprints.
* **Nanostructure Characterization:** Localized organic nanostructures (sugar clusters) were identified with volumes ranging from $12$ to $567\text{ nm}^3$ and cluster sizes between 4.36 and 15.78 nm.
* **Mass Spectrometry Trends:** Honey mass spectra behave more similarly to bulk glucose than aqueous solutions, suggesting that high sugar concentrations induce a macroscopic transition from liquid-like to solid-like behavior.

## Methods And Instrumentation Notes
* **Sample Preparation & Transfer:** Honey was loaded onto pre-roughened tungsten wires via dipping and rapidly quenched in liquid $N_2$. A Leica VCT500/T500 cryo-transfer system was used at $-184^\circ\text{C}$ under low pressure ($10^{-4}\text{ mbar}$) to prevent dehydration.
* **FIB Micromachining:** Tips ($r < 100\text{--}200\text{ nm}$) were prepared using an FEI Scios FIB with a custom cryo-stage ($-150^\circ\text{C}$). An annular milling pattern with a decreasing current strategy ($15\text{ nA}$ down to $0.1\text{ pA}$) was employed. SEM imaging was performed at low energy ($5\text{ kV}$, $25\text{ pA}$) to prevent specimen melting.
* **APT Parameters:** 
    * **Hardware:** Custom-built atom probe with a $355\text{ nm}$ UV laser (250 fs pulse, 50 µm spot size) and a $120\text{ mm}$ diameter delay line detector (50% OAR).
    * **Conditions:** Pulse rate of 100 kHz; laser energies 40–80 nJ; base temperature of 60 K.
    * **Detection:** Achieved a detection rate of 300–400 atoms/s ($\approx$0.003 atoms/pulse).
* **Data Processing:** Reconstructions were performed using Scito69 software (Bas et al. point projection method). To mitigate surface contamination, the first 10 million atoms were discarded. Analysis included Nearest-Neighbor (NN) analysis, Cluster Search Algorithms, and isocomposition surfaces.

## Relevance To APT/FIM
* **Cryo-APT Capability:** Demonstrates that new cryo-transfer systems allow for the study of aqueous/soft matter in their natural frozen state rather than being limited to crystalline or resin-embedded forms.
* **Mass Spectrometry Challenges:** Highlights significant mass spectrometry overlap between common sugar fragments ($C_xH_y$ and $C_xO_yH_z$) and water clusters ($(H_2O)_n$), requiring specialized interpretation models for stoichiometry.
* **Evaporation Physics:** Illustrates that unlike metals, organic materials undergo complex fragmentation/dissociation during evaporation. It also notes the presence of multi-hit events (8.92% double, 4.2% triple hits) which must be managed to prevent biased reconstruction.

## Open Questions
* **Algorithm Calibration:** There is a documented need to calibrate and modify reconstruction algorithms specifically for aqueous/cryogenic samples to ensure local and chemical resolution accuracy.
* **Isomer Differentiation:** The technique currently cannot distinguish between isomers with identical chemical formulas (e.g., glucose vs. fructose).
* **Quantification Uncertainties:** Absolute water content remains highly uncertain due to overlapping $m/q$ peaks and ambiguities in charge states (e.g., $C_3^{2+}$ vs. $H_2O^+$ at 18 u). Future use of kinetic energy-sensitive detectors may be required.
* **Unquantified Fields:** The exact evaporation fields for sugars remain unquantified, making conclusions regarding bonding strength under high-field conditions partially speculative.
