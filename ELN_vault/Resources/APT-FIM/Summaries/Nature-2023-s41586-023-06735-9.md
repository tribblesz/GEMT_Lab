# Nature-2023-s41586-023-06735-9

## Source PDF

[[Resources/APT-FIM/PDFs/Nature-2023-s41586-023-06735-9.pdf]]

## Overview
The study introduces **GNoME** (Graph Networks for Materials Exploration), an active learning pipeline designed to predict the stability of inorganic crystals. The framework utilizes a "data flywheel" approach, where Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) filter candidate structures generated via symmetry-aware partial substitutions (SAPS) and random searches, followed by verification through Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations to iteratively retrain the models.

## Key Findings
* **Scale of Discovery:** The framework expanded the known universe of stable crystals from ~48,000 to 421,000 structures, identifying approximately 381,000 new stable crystal structures and 45,500 novel prototypes.
* **Model Performance:** Through six rounds of active learning, prediction error was reduced from 28 meV/atom to $11\text{ meV/atom}$, with structural prediction hit rates increasing from $<6\%$ to $>80\%$.
* **Stability Validation:** Using the r2SCAN meta-GGA functional, the study confirmed that 84% of discovered binaries/ternaries and 86.8% of quaternaries maintain negative phase-separation energies.
* **Generalization & Transferability:** The models demonstrated "zero-shot" prediction capabilities for unseen compositions (e.g., superionic conductors) and high transferability to extreme conditions, such as predicting behavior at 1,000K using data trained only at 400K.
* **Target Material Classes:** Identified high-priority candidates include Li-ion conductors, layered materials, and Li/Mn transition-metal oxides.

## Methods And Instrumentation Notes
* **Discovery Pipelines:** Two parallel tracks were used: a **structural pipeline** (using SAPS to modify existing crystals) and a **compositional pipeline** (predicting stability from reduced chemical formulas).
* **Computational Frameworks:** 
    * **Machine Learning:** GNNs employing message-passing neural networks (MLPs with swish nonlinearities), NequIP interatomic potentials (implemented in JAX), and Deep Ensembles ($n=10$) for uncertainty quantification.
    * **DFT & Search:** VASP (using PBE and r2SCAN functionals) for stability validation; Ab Initio Random Structure Searching (AIRSS) for generating initial structures; `pymatgen` for symmetry analysis and diffusion analysis.
* **Simulation Parameters:** 520 eV plane-wave basis cutoff; interatomic distance cutoffs of 4.0 Å (structural models) and 5.0 Å (potentials); 2-fs time steps for AIMD/MLMD simulations.
* **Error Mitigation:** Implementation of Test-Time Augmentation (TTA) via isotropic lattice scaling (80% to 120% volume) and deterministic MD5 hashing to prevent data leakage between training and test sets.

## Relevance To APT/FIM
While purely computational, GNoME provides a high-fidelity library of predicted stable phases that serve as **high-priority targets for experimental characterization**. 
* **Target Identification:** The predicted multi-element systems and layered materials are primary candidates for Atom Probe Tomography (APT) and Field Ion Microscopy (FIM) to verify predicted stoichiometry, local chemical partitioning, and vacancy distribution.
* **Mechanistic Modeling:** The framework's ability to predict species-specific diffusivity and kinetic properties provides a theoretical counterpart to the atomistic-scale motion, ion migration pathways, and cluster formation observed during field evaporation in 3D atom probe reconstructions.

## Open Questions
* **Synthesizability:** A significant gap remains regarding the transition from computational prediction to experimental reality (i.e., whether these structures can be physically synthesized).
* **Dynamic Stability:** The model does not yet fully account for dynamic stability (vibrational entropy) or phase transitions involving competing polymorphs.
* **Convergence & Accuracy:** The underlying cause of DFT relaxation convergence failures for certain compositions remains unidentified, and accuracy in compositional predictions decreases when AIRSS simulations fail to converge.
* **Label Noise:** There is an inherent challenge regarding label noise, as compositional models assume training labels represent ground-state phases, which is not always guaranteed.
