# Atom Probe Tomographic Mapping of Phosphorus in Resin-Embedded Ferritin - Supplement

## Source PDF

[[Resources/APT-FIM/PDFs/Atom Probe Tomographic Mapping of Phosphorus in Resin-Embedded Ferritin - Supplement.pdf]]

## Overview
The study demonstrates the use of Atom Probe Tomally (APT) to map the atomic distribution of phosphorus (P) within ferritin molecules embedded in a lowicryl organic polymer resin matrix. The research also evaluates the reconstruction of iron (Fe) distribution and validates morphological findings using $\text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4$ nanoparticles.

## Key Findings
* **Morphological Artifacts:** Reconstructed Fe cores exhibit an ellipsoidal rather than spherical shape, with elongation oriented normal to the analysis direction. This was also observed in $\text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4$ nanoparticles and is attributed to "evaporation bursts" caused by the electric field disparity between Fe and the organic polymer.
* **Analytical Consistency:** Despite variations in ferritin-to-ferritin spacing, compositional profiles for Fe, $\text{FePO}_2$, P, and C remain spatially consistent across different specimens when averaged over multiple molecules.
* **Density-Induced Artifacts:** High particle density within reconstructions can produce non-physical signals, such as apparent Na sequestration in a ~5nm shell beyond the protein and N profiles that fail to drop to background levels at the protein-resin interface (despite the resin being N-free).

## Methods And Instrumentation Notes
* **Instrumentation:** Specimen collection/evaporation was performed using UV laser-assisted APT with a pulse energy of 450 pJ.
* **Analytical Method:** A "difference spectra" approach was used, subtracting the mass spectrum of pure resin from the ferritin-embedded specimen to isolate biological chemical signals.
* **Reconstruction Parameters:** 15% $^{56}\text{Fe}^+$ isoconcentration surfaces were used to define Fe-rich regions. Bounding boxes utilized included $66 \times 65 \times 56 \text{ nm}^3$ and $48 \times 48 \times 46 \text{ nm}^3$.
* **Mass Spectrometry Challenges:** Significant isobaric interference exists for P and N. Phosphorus detection relies on identifying peaks such as $m/z$ 15.49 ($P^{2+}$), 30.97 ($P^{+}$), and 46.97 ($PO^{+}$), which are confounded by nitrogen-carbon-hydrogen clusters. High-order molecular clusters (e.g., $m/z$ 206.82 for $Fe_2PO_4^{+}$) are essential for mapping Fe and P distribution.

## Relevance To APT/FIM
* **Field Disparity:** The study highlights how the significant difference between the high electric field required for Fe evaporation and the much lower field required for organic polymers causes reconstruction aberrations (evaporation bursts).
* **Interface Analysis:** Demonstrates the ability to map elemental distributions at biological-organic interfaces, while cautioning that high particle density can obscure true distributions and create false signals of element migration or sequestration.
